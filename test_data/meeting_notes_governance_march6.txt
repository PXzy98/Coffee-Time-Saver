------------------------------------------------------------
AGENDA ITEM 1 — Purpose of Meeting (09:00)
------------------------------------------------------------

DG opened by clarifying scope of the discussion:
  "Today isn't about approving a finished solution. It's about determining whether
   exploring this concept is responsible and worthwhile under our constraints."

PM Director framed the problem statement:
  The challenge is fragmentation — decisions, risks, and commitments are distributed
  across emails, transcripts, documents, and slide decks. The manual effort required
  to synthesize this information before governance meetings and key decisions is
  increasing and is a recognized efficiency gap.

Technical Authority noted this is about augmentation, not automation:
  The objective is reducing cognitive overhead for PMs and decision-makers — not
  removing human judgment from governance processes.

Enterprise Architect clarified the technical premise:
  The prototype tests whether structured analysis across unstructured, exported data
  can surface useful signals. This is not an infrastructure modernization initiative.


------------------------------------------------------------
AGENDA ITEM 2 — Feasibility and Prototype Approach (09:08)
------------------------------------------------------------

DG acknowledged uncertainty:
  "We don't know if this will deliver meaningful value. That's what the prototype is for."

Enterprise Architect on expected outcomes:
  - We suspect value exists in inconsistency detection and dependency surfacing
  - Prototype is explicitly designed to answer whether that value is real and measurable
  - Partial success (demonstrated in controlled conditions) is sufficient to justify
    refinement investment

Technical Authority:
  Outputs must be traceable and evidence-based. Even partial success justifies further work.
  Failure modes are also informative — knowing limitations of this approach in our environment
  is valuable data.

ACTION: Enterprise Architect to circulate architecture design summary by March 10.


------------------------------------------------------------
AGENDA ITEM 3 — Responsible AI and Guardrails (09:13)
------------------------------------------------------------

Privacy Advisor requirements:
  - Clear separation between extracted evidence and AI interpretation
  - Outputs must show the evidence first; interpretation must be clearly labeled
  - Human validation step required before any AI output informs a decision

IT Security Lead requirements:
  - Architecture must assume NO live integrations
  - No real-time data feeds
  - Full audit logging of: inputs, processing steps, outputs
  - Sandbox environment only — no connection to production systems

Enterprise Architect confirmed these are already built into the design:
  - Manual data exports only
  - Sandbox-only deployment
  - Evidence-first output format
  - Human validation gate before decision use

DG summarized the principle:
  "Augmentation with accountability."

NOTE: Legal Counsel's written comments also requested documentation of data handling
procedures and confirmation that no personal information will be processed in the prototype.
Enterprise Architect to confirm in architecture document.


------------------------------------------------------------
AGENDA ITEM 4 — Strategic Value Assessment (09:18)
------------------------------------------------------------

PM Director on expected operational benefits:
  - Reduced time spent manually reviewing documentation before governance reviews
  - Earlier visibility into misalignment between documented commitments and actual
    project status

Technical Authority:
  Even identifying what doesn't work in our environment has value — helps us scope
  future tooling decisions more accurately.

DG cautioned against overstating outcomes:
  "We should measure success by demonstrated insight — not perfection. If it surfaces
   one meaningful inconsistency that would otherwise have been missed, that's a
   data point worth having."


------------------------------------------------------------
AGENDA ITEM 5 — Decision and Next Steps (09:22)
------------------------------------------------------------

DECISION (DG): Proceed with controlled prototype under the following guardrails:

  GUARDRAIL 1: Evidence-first outputs — all surfaced items must cite source document
               and include extracted evidence snippet before any interpretation

  GUARDRAIL 2: No automation of decision authority — human validation required before
               any AI-generated output is used to inform a governance decision

  GUARDRAIL 3: Security and lifecycle documentation — threat model and ITSG-33
               alignment documentation must be included in prototype deliverables

  GUARDRAIL 4: Success measured by demonstrated insight — project team to define
               minimum success criteria before prototype demonstration

  "Let's move forward with disciplined experimentation."
