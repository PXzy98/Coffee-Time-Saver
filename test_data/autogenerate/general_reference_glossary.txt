PROJECT MANAGEMENT GLOSSARY — INTERNAL REFERENCE
Version 2.1  |  Issued by: PMO Centre of Excellence  |  Date: January 2026

This glossary defines standard terminology used across all projects governed by the Project Management Office (PMO). It is a reference document only and does not contain project-specific commitments, action items, or deadlines.

---

AGILE
An iterative approach to project delivery that emphasises flexibility, collaboration, and incremental releases. Work is organised in short cycles (sprints) of 1–4 weeks.

BASELINE
The approved version of a project plan (schedule, scope, or budget) used as a reference point for measuring progress and managing change.

BUSINESS CASE
A formal document that justifies the initiation of a project by describing the problem or opportunity, proposed solution, costs, benefits, risks, and strategic alignment.

CHANGE CONTROL BOARD (CCB)
A group of stakeholders authorised to review and approve or reject changes to the project's scope, schedule, or budget. All Change Requests must be reviewed by the CCB before implementation.

CHANGE REQUEST (CR)
A formal proposal to modify any aspect of a project baseline. Must include impact assessment on scope, cost, and schedule.

CRITICAL PATH
The longest sequence of dependent tasks in a project schedule. A delay on the critical path delays the entire project.

DELIVERABLE
A tangible output produced by a project — a document, software release, infrastructure component, or physical item — that satisfies a project requirement.

EARNED VALUE MANAGEMENT (EVM)
A technique for measuring project performance by comparing planned work (Planned Value), completed work (Earned Value), and actual costs (Actual Cost).

GATE REVIEW
A structured checkpoint at a defined phase of a project where a decision authority (Steering Committee or sponsor) reviews progress and decides whether to proceed, pause, or cancel.

ISSUE
A current problem or concern that is already affecting the project and requires resolution. Distinct from a risk, which is a potential future event.

LESSONS LEARNED
Documentation of what went well and what went poorly during a project or project phase, intended to improve future projects.

MILESTONE
A significant event in the project schedule, typically marking the completion of a major deliverable or phase. Milestones have zero duration.

PMO (Project Management Office)
The organisational unit responsible for defining and maintaining project management standards, providing governance oversight, and supporting project managers.

PRINCE2
A structured project management methodology widely used in government and public sector projects. Defines seven principles, seven themes, and seven processes.

PROCUREMENT
The process of acquiring goods or services from external vendors, including tendering, evaluation, contract award, and contract management.

RACI MATRIX
A responsibility assignment matrix that defines who is Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, and Informed for each task or deliverable.

RISK
An uncertain event that, if it occurs, will have a positive or negative effect on the project objectives. Each risk has a probability and an impact.

RISK REGISTER
A log of identified risks, including their description, probability, impact, risk rating, mitigation strategies, and risk owner.

SCOPE CREEP
The uncontrolled expansion of project scope without corresponding adjustments to schedule, budget, or resources.

SPRINT
In Agile/Scrum, a fixed-length iteration (typically 1–4 weeks) during which a defined set of work is completed and reviewed.

STAKEHOLDER
Any person, group, or organisation that may affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by the project.

WATERFALL
A sequential project delivery approach where phases (requirements, design, build, test, deploy) are completed one at a time before moving to the next.

WORK BREAKDOWN STRUCTURE (WBS)
A hierarchical decomposition of the project's total scope into manageable work packages. The WBS defines the full deliverable tree for the project.

---

END OF GLOSSARY
